# Speaking

I talk about portable systems, contract-driven AI development, and what it actually takes to restructure software for the age of AI agents. Every talk is drawn directly from published books, white papers, and shipped tooling.

<ep-talk-card title="Write Once, Run Where It Makes Sense" track="Architecture and platform engineering" description="The same business logic gets rewritten four or five times across browser, backend, edge, and AI pipelines. Most architectures assume the environment too early and teams pay that cost forever. This talk covers the architecture of portable business logic with WebAssembly and Universal Microservices Architecture — what it means to separate logic from execution, and why it matters more now than ever." based-on="Universal Microservices Architecture" based-on-url="https://www.amazon.com/dp/B0GTTTTQH4"></ep-talk-card>

<ep-talk-card title="Contract-Driven AI Development" track="AI engineering and developer tooling" description="AI coding tools can describe what software does but not why it does it. The difference between a specification and a contract is the difference between automation and trust." based-on="C-DAD White Paper" based-on-url="/whitepapers/Contract-Driven AI Development (C-DAD) - White Paper.pdf"></ep-talk-card>

<ep-talk-card title="The Day After: Restructuring Software for the Age of AI Agents" track="Engineering leadership and CTO tracks" description="Most codebases are not navigable by AI agents because the intent was never declared. This talk covers practical patterns for making software organizations legible to AI agents without throwing everything away — role by role, capability by capability." based-on="The Day After (forthcoming) and C-DAD White Paper" based-on-url="/whitepapers/Contract-Driven AI Development (C-DAD) - White Paper.pdf"></ep-talk-card>

Enrico Piovesan is a platform architect and author who builds frameworks for software that runs anywhere and codebases that AI agents can actually navigate. After years building products across startups in travel, education, and payments, he developed Universal Microservices Architecture — a portable, contract-driven execution model for distributed systems. His first book on UMA is available on Amazon. His second book, The Day After, explores what it takes to restructure software organizations for the age of AI agents. He is a Platform Software Architect at Autodesk, an active open source builder, and publishes on architecture and AI-native systems every week on Medium. He is based in Golden, BC, Purcell Mountains, Canada.
